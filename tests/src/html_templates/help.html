What is this?

ContextFreeParsing(101) is a tool to help you learn about Context Free Grammars -- if you don't know what's that, please refer to the Grammars tab, or to the Wikipedia page . The app is inspired by Regex101.com (if you're not familiar with that site, I can't recommend it enough), but is focused on context free grammars, instead of regexes. Of course it's not half as good as the other one, that's why the 101 is in the braces.

What can it do?

The app allows you to enter a grammar in the Backus-Naur Form, checks whether it is formally correct (no common symbols in terminals and nonterminals etc.) and produces a visualization of its rules and a parsing of given input string.

Entering the grammar

Just fill in the form! A terminal is considered as any combination of the alphanumeric characters and _. Terminals are separated by whitespaces and are case-sensitive:

Nonterminals are exactly the same, except they must be surrounded with diamond braces:

The rules are entered in the Backus-Naur form. A new rule starts with a new line, but if you want to break a single rule over multiple lines (perhaps to increase clarity) use the backslash (\):
Rules

Visualization

The app attempts to help you visualize the grammar by producing a tree of derrivable strings (of both terminals and nonterminals). There are 2 modes of visualization: simple, where each rule is used once in the tree, and complex, where each rule is matched once in each path from the root to the leaves.
